    To determine the gender of the child on the Japanese calendar, you need:

    1. In the first table, find the column with the month of birth of the mother and the row with the month of birth of the father.
    2. Remember the number at the intersection of the column and row
    3. In the second table, find the row with the number from the first table and the column with the month of conception. At their intersection, the gender of the future baby is indicated and the probability of the correctness of the forecast is indicated. Both genders are indicated in some cells, which means that the conception of a boy and a girl is equally probable.

    Japanese gender planning

    If you look closely at the table, you can see that some months are marked by a high probability of giving birth to a boy or girl, while others have an approximately equal odds ratio. If a couple dreams of a child of a certain gender, you should choose the months with the greatest difference in probability for conceiving a child. For example, for a couple to whom the number 7 in the first table corresponds, the greatest chances to conceive a boy at 2 and 10 months of the year, and a girl at 3 and 11.

    The accuracy of the Japanese baby chart

    The Japanese baby chart has long been popular in many countries around the world. In Asia, she is considered the most accurate way to predict the gender of the child.

    Reviews of the Japanese baby chart from forums and social media groups suggest that about 80% of forecasts are correct.

    What is the basis for determining gender by a Japanese baby chart?

    The gender of the child depends on the set of chromosomes that the sperm contains. In women, the egg always has the same set of chromosomes, but male cells can carry both the male combination of chromosomes and the female. This is a proven scientific fact.

    Modern medicine claims that it is impossible to predict in advance which set of chromosomes the sperm that have fertilized the egg will carry. The opposite opinion was expressed by a group of Japanese scientists. They believe that in some time cycles, the most active are male cells with a male set of chromosomes, and in others, with a female one. This assumption served as the basis for the compilation of the Japanese pregnancy calendar.
